## How to Run for Office

### General Process
1. **Decide Which Office**: Consider your experience, time commitment, and passion
2. **Meet Requirements**: Age, residency, party affiliation (if partisan)
3. **File Declaration**: File with county clerk or secretary of state (depending on office)
4. **Pay Filing Fee**: Varies by office ($0 to several hundred dollars)
5. **Collect Signatures**: Some offices require petition signatures
6. **Run in Primary**: If partisan office (May election)
7. **General Election**: November

### Key Deadlines
- **Federal/State Offices**: Filing usually opens in January, closes early February
- **County Offices**: Filing opens January, closes February
- **Municipal Offices**: Filing opens late winter/early spring
- **School Boards**: Non-partisan, file in summer for November odd-year election

### Resources
- **Indiana Election Division**: [in.gov/sos/elections](https://www.in.gov/sos/elections/)
- **Hamilton County Clerk**: Election information and candidate filing
- **County Party Organizations**: Training and support for candidates

## Term Limits Summary

### Federal
- **President**: 2 terms (8 years maximum)
- **U.S. Senate**: No term limits
- **U.S. House**: No term limits

### State
- **Governor/Lt. Governor**: 2 consecutive terms (8 years, can run again after sitting out)
- **Other Statewide Officers**: No term limits
- **State Legislature**: No term limits

### County/Local
- **No term limits**: Most county and local offices have no term limits in Indiana
- **Exception**: Some cities may have mayoral term limits by local ordinance
