# U.S. Representative

## Overview
Members of the U.S. House of Representatives are elected from congressional districts. Hamilton County is in **Indiana's 5th Congressional District**.

## Requirements
- At least 25 years old
- U.S. citizen for at least 7 years
- Resident of the state (Indiana)
- Traditionally resident of the district

## Election Process
- Elected by congressional district
- Partisan election
- **Indiana has 9 congressional districts**
- Districts redrawn every 10 years after census

## Term
- **Length:** 2 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** Every 2 years in even-numbered years
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Member of U.S. House of Representatives (435 total members)
- Represents congressional district (Hamilton County in 5th District)
- Passes federal legislation
- Initiates revenue (tax) bills (House exclusive power)
- Declares war (Congress)
- Controls federal budget and appropriations
- Impeaches federal officials (House initiates, Senate tries)
- Representation based on state population
- Committee work and hearings
- Constituent services for district residents
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 60-80+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily when House in session; Committee hearings; Caucus meetings; Constituent meetings; Fundraising; Town halls in district

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Revenue bills** - Must originate in House (exclusive)
- **Impeachment** - House brings charges (exclusive)
- **Legislative authority** - Pass federal laws
- **War declaration** authority (Congress)
- **Budget and appropriations** control
- **Electoral College tie** - House elects president (1 vote per state)
- **Population representation** - More representatives for larger states

**Procedural Powers:**
- Majority party controls all committees and floor action
- Speaker of House has enormous power (2nd in line to presidency)
- Committee chairs control hearings and investigations
- Rules Committee controls which bills reach floor
- Can attach amendments and riders to bills
- Platform for higher office (Senate, Governor)
- Shorter terms mean constant campaigning and fundraising
- Media attention for district priorities
- Can bring federal projects/funding to district (earmarks)
- Party leadership positions multiply influence
- Relationship with president affects district priorities
- Can force votes to embarrass opponents

## Indiana's 5th Congressional District
- **Covers:** Hamilton County (entirely), parts of Marion County and surrounding areas
- **Current Representative:** Victoria Spartz (R) - Since 2021
- Includes: Carmel, Fishers, Noblesville, Westfield, northern Indianapolis suburbs
