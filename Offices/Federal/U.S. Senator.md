# U.S. Senator from Indiana

## Requirements
- At least 30 years old
- U.S. citizen for at least 9 years
- Resident of Indiana

## Election Process
- Elected statewide
- Partisan election
- **Indiana has 2 U.S. Senators**
- Terms are staggered

## Term
- **Length:** 6 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** Every 6 years (staggered between the two seats)

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Member of U.S. Senate (100 total senators, 2 per state)
- Represents entire state of Indiana
- Passes federal legislation
- Confirms presidential appointments (judges, cabinet, ambassadors)
- Ratifies treaties (requires 2/3 vote)
- Tries impeachments (Senate only)
- Declares war (Congress)
- Controls federal budget and appropriations
- Equal representation for each state (2 senators regardless of population)
- Committee work and hearings
- Constituent services for Hoosiers
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 60-80+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily when Senate in session; Committee hearings; Caucus meetings; Constituent meetings; Fundraising; Town halls in Indiana

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Advice and consent** - Confirms judges, cabinet, ambassadors
- **Treaty ratification** - 2/3 vote required
- **Impeachment trial** - Senate serves as jury
- **Legislative authority** - Pass federal laws
- **War declaration** authority (Congress)
- **Budget and appropriations** control
- **Filibuster** - Can speak indefinitely (Senate tradition)
- **Equal state representation** - Small state power

**Procedural Powers:**
- Filibuster requires 60 votes to overcome (massive leverage)
- Single senator can block nominees via hold
- Committee chairs control hearings and investigations
- Seniority affects committee assignments and influence
- Can attach amendments to any bill
- Platform for national issues and presidential runs
- Fundraising ability affects party power
- Media attention provides national platform
- Can investigate executive branch through committees
- Unanimous consent procedures allow one senator to block
- Party leadership positions multiply influence
- Relationship with president affects Indiana priorities
