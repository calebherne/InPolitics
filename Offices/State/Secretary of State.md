# Secretary of State of Indiana

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen
- Resident of Indiana
- At least 18 years old (typical constitutional requirement)

## Election Process
- Elected statewide on partisan ballot
- Independent election
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None specified
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Current Office Holder
**[Diego Morales](../../Profiles/State/Secretary%20of%20State%20-%20Diego%20Morales.md)** (Republican)
- **Assumed Office:** January 1, 2023
- **Previous Experience:** Indiana State Senate staff, businessman

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Chief election officer
- Oversees voter registration systems statewide
- Certifies election results
- Processes business entity registrations and filings
- Maintains state archives and records
- Regulates securities dealers and offerings
- Oversees auto dealer licensing
- Authenticates official documents (apostilles)
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Regular with county clerks, business community, election officials

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Chief election officer** - Oversees all election administration
- **Business registration authority** - Approves/rejects filings
- **Securities regulation** - Enforcement of securities laws
- **Official document authentication**
- **Preservation of state records**

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can set election procedures and interpret election law
- Discretion in investigating election complaints
- Can use office to advocate for election law changes
- Controls business filing requirements and fees
- May delay or expedite business registrations
- Platform for advocating voting access or restrictions
- Influence over counties through election guidance
- Can refer securities violations for prosecution
