# Governor of Indiana

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen for at least 5 years
- Resident of Indiana for at least 5 years
- At least 30 years old

## Election Process
- Elected statewide on a partisan ballot
- Runs on a joint ticket with [Lieutenant Governor](Lieutenant%20Governor.md)
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** Can serve no more than 8 years in a 12-year period
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Salaried executive role
- Chief executive officer managing state government
- Delivers State of the State address annually
- Proposes biennial state budget to legislature
- Signs or vetoes legislation passed by General Assembly
- Nominates candidates for judicial appointments through commission process
- Issues executive orders to state agencies
- Calls special legislative sessions when needed
- Represents Indiana in interstate and federal matters
- Responds to state emergencies and disasters
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 60-80+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily with staff, cabinet, legislators, stakeholders

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Veto power** over legislation (simple majority override - relatively weak)
- **Line-item veto** on appropriations bills
- **Appointment power** for executive branch positions and judicial nominees
- **Pardon and commutation** authority
- **Commander-in-chief** of Indiana National Guard
- **Emergency powers** during declared emergencies

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can use executive orders to reorganize agencies without legislative approval
- Emergency declaration grants broad temporary authority over state resources
- Controls timing of special sessions and their agenda
- Bully pulpit to shape public opinion and legislative priorities
- Pocket veto by not acting on bills before session ends
- Can threaten veto to influence bill negotiations
- Controls executive branch hiring and patronage jobs
