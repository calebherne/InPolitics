# State Convention Delegate (Republican Party of Indiana)

## Overview
State Convention Delegates are elected representatives who attend the Indiana Republican State Convention to vote on party business, elect party leadership, and participate in the presidential nomination process.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by precinct committeemen at district caucuses
- Must be a registered Republican voter in Indiana
- Must reside in the district represented
- Typically must have voted in the most recent Republican primary

## How Elected
- Elected at district caucuses held after the primary election
- Precinct committeemen from each district meet to elect delegates
- Number of delegates per district varies based on Republican voter turnout in previous elections
- Each district typically elects multiple delegates proportional to its Republican strength

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **State Convention Attendance**: Attend the biennial State Convention (held in June of even-numbered years)
- **Leadership Elections**: Vote to elect State Party Chair, Vice Chair, Secretary, Treasurer
- **National Representatives**: Vote to elect National Committeeman and National Committeewoman (every 4 years)
- **Platform Adoption**: Vote on proposed changes to state party platform
- **Rules and Bylaws**: Vote on changes to party rules and procedures
- **Presidential Delegates**: Help select delegates to Republican National Convention (presidential years)
- **Resolutions**: Consider and vote on resolutions affecting party positions

**Time Commitment**: Minimal - primarily the convention itself
- State Convention (typically 1-2 days in June)
- Pre-convention meetings or caucuses (optional)
- Presidential nomination years may require additional time

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Vote on all matters before the State Convention
- Elect state party leadership
- Adopt or amend party platform
- Approve changes to party rules and bylaws
- Select delegates to Republican National Convention (presidential years)
- Pass resolutions directing party priorities

### Limitations:
- Authority only during State Convention
- Cannot make decisions for the party between conventions (that's the State Committee's role)
- Bound by existing party rules unless amended through proper process
- One vote per delegate (equal voting power regardless of district size)

### Rarely Used Powers:
- Can propose new party rules or platform planks from the floor
- Technically can remove party officers through vote of no confidence
- Can override State Committee decisions through convention action

## Term Length
- 2-year term (until next State Convention)
- Separate election process each cycle
- Must be re-elected to serve multiple terms
- Automatic vacancy if delegate moves out of district or changes party registration

## Current Office Holders
Hundreds of delegates across Indiana's various districts (elected at district caucuses after each primary election)
