# State Auditor of Indiana

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen
- Resident of Indiana
- At least 18 years old (typical constitutional requirement)

## Election Process
- Elected statewide on partisan ballot
- Independent election
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None specified
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Current Office Holder
**[Elise Nieshalla](../../Profiles/State/State%20Auditor%20-%20Elise%20Nieshalla.md)** (Republican)
- **Assumed Office:** November 30, 2023 (appointed)
- **Previous Experience:** State financial management roles

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Chief fiscal watchdog
- Audits state agencies and local governments
- Processes state payments and receipts
- Maintains state accounting records
- Issues warrants for state payments
- Reviews property tax systems
- Publishes financial reports
- Pre-audits expenditures for legality
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Regular with agencies, local officials, audit staff; public hearings on findings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Audit authority** over all state and local government entities
- **Payment approval** - Must warrant all state expenditures
- **Financial reporting** - Official state fiscal records
- **Property tax oversight**
- Can require information from any government entity

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can choose audit targets and timing (political implications)
- Discretion in audit depth and public report content
- Can delay payments by questioning legality
- Platform to expose waste, fraud, or mismanagement
- Can refer findings for prosecution
- Audit findings can damage political opponents
- Controls narrative around state finances
- Can advocate for budget reforms based on findings
- May withhold payments pending legal review
