# Indiana Court of Appeals

## Requirements
- Licensed attorney
- Admitted to practice law in Indiana
- U.S. citizen

## Selection Process
- **NOT elected** - Appointed through merit selection
- Indiana Judicial Nominating Commission nominates candidates
- [Governor](Governor.md) appoints from nominees
- Retention elections every 10 years after initial appointment

## Term
- **Initial Term:** Until first retention election
- **Retention:** 10-year terms if retained by voters
- **Mandatory Retirement:** Age 75

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Professional appellate judge
- Reviews trial court records and briefs
- Hears oral arguments (in panels of 3)
- Writes appellate opinions
- Sits in rotating 3-judge panels
- Reviews administrative agency decisions
- Studies legal issues and precedents
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Panel conferences several times weekly; Oral argument sessions; Judicial education

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Appellate review** of trial court decisions
- **Error correction** - Reverse improper rulings
- **Precedent creation** (unless overturned by Supreme Court)
- **Administrative review** of agency decisions
- Can remand cases for new trial
- Sits in **panels of 3 judges** (15 total judges)

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can interpret statutes in ways that shape policy
- Published opinions create binding precedent on lower courts
- Can expedite or delay case decisions
- Strategic opinion writing to invite Supreme Court review
- Can signal disagreement with precedent to encourage change
- Unpublished opinions avoid creating precedent
- Panel composition affects case outcomes
