# District Chair (Republican Party)

## Overview
District Chairs lead the Republican Party organization within Indiana's congressional districts, coordinating party activities and serving as a liaison between county parties and state party leadership.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by precinct committeemen within the district
- Must be a registered Republican voter in Indiana
- Must reside within the congressional district
- No specific age or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected at district caucus held after primary election
- Precinct committeemen from all precincts in the congressional district vote
- Typically elected every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of precinct committeemen present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **District Coordination**: Coordinate Republican Party activities across multiple counties within the district
- **Congressional Campaigns**: Support Republican congressional candidate in district elections
- **County Liaison**: Serve as connection between county party chairs and state party
- **State Committee Member**: Serve as voting member of State Committee
- **District Events**: Organize district-level party events and fundraisers
- **Voter Outreach**: Coordinate voter registration and turnout efforts within district
- **Caucus Leadership**: Preside over district caucuses to elect convention delegates

**Time Commitment**: Part-time volunteer position
- Monthly or quarterly district meetings
- State Committee meetings (typically quarterly)
- District caucus after primary elections
- Increased activity during campaign seasons
- Coordination calls with county chairs

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Vote on State Committee decisions affecting entire state party
- Preside over district caucuses
- Coordinate resources and strategy within district
- Speak on behalf of district Republicans
- Access to state party resources and data for district
- Input on state party strategy and priorities

### Limitations:
- Cannot override decisions of county party chairs within their counties
- Limited authority over county party operations
- Subject to state party rules and State Committee
- Typically no direct control over party funds (unless district has separate account)
- Cannot discipline or remove precinct committeemen or county officers

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
- Term runs from district caucus until next caucus after subsequent primary
