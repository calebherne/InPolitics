# Lieutenant Governor of Indiana

## Requirements
- Same as [Governor](Governor.md)
- U.S. citizen for at least 5 years
- Resident of Indiana for at least 5 years
- At least 30 years old

## Election Process
- Elected jointly with [Governor](Governor.md) on same ticket
- Cannot run separately
- Elected statewide on partisan ballot
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years (same as Governor)
- **Term Limits:** Follows Governor's term limits
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Roles
- **Part-time to full-time** depending on Governor's delegation
- Presides over Senate sessions (ceremonial unless tie vote)
- Serves as Commissioner of Agriculture
- Chairs or serves on various state boards and commissions assigned by Governor
- Acts as Governor when Governor is out of state or incapacitated
- Public appearances and ribbon-cuttings as assigned
- **Time Commitment:** 20-40 hours/week typically, more with added responsibilities
- **Meetings:** Senate sessions during legislative session, board meetings, events

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Tie-breaking vote** in State Senate (50-50 ties)
- **Succession** - Becomes Governor if office vacant
- **Acting Governor** powers when Governor absent
- Presides over Senate impeachment trials (non-Governor impeachments)

**Procedural Powers:**
- As Senate President, controls floor recognition (can influence debate timing)
- Can use tie-breaking vote strategically on crucial 25-25 votes
- Position provides platform for future gubernatorial run
- Access to Governor's inner circle and information
- Can delay or expedite matters as presiding officer
- May leverage assigned portfolio (Agriculture) for political influence
