# Attorney General of Indiana

## Requirements
- Licensed attorney in Indiana
- Must be admitted to practice law in Indiana

## Election Process
- Elected statewide on partisan ballot
- Independent of [[Governor]]
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None specified
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Current Office Holder
**[Todd Rokita](../../Profiles/State/Attorney%20General%20-%20Todd%20Rokita.md)** (Republican)
- **Assumed Office:** January 11, 2021
- **Previous Experience:** U.S. Representative (2011-2019), Indiana Secretary of State (2002-2010)

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - State's chief legal officer
- Provides legal counsel to state agencies and officials
- Reviews and approves legal opinions for agencies
- Represents state in lawsuits and appeals
- Defends state laws challenged in court
- Prosecutes Medicaid fraud cases
- Enforces consumer protection laws
- Manages legal staff and AG office operations
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Regular with agency heads, prosecutors, staff; court appearances

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Legal representation** of state in all official matters
- **Authority to prosecute** violations of state law (concurrent with local prosecutors)
- **Advisory opinions** binding on state agencies
- **Consumer protection enforcement** including civil actions
- **Medicaid fraud prosecution**
- **Antitrust enforcement**

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can choose which cases to personally argue vs. delegate
- Discretion in interpreting laws through advisory opinions
- Can decline to defend certain state laws (though controversial)
- Multistate litigation coordination and leadership
- Can initiate investigations without governor approval
- Controls litigation strategy that affects state policy
- Standing to challenge federal actions affecting Indiana
- Can use office as platform for higher political office
