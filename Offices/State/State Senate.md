# Indiana State Senate

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen
- Resident of Indiana for at least 2 years
- Resident of the district at time of election
- At least 25 years old

## Election Process
- Elected by district on partisan ballot
- **Total Seats:** 50 senators
- Districts redrawn every 10 years after census

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** Staggered - half the Senate elected every 2 years

## Roles
- **Part-time position** - Indiana has citizen legislature
- Participates in legislative session (typically January-April)
- Reviews and debates legislation
- Serves on committees (ongoing work)
- Constituent services year-round
- Attends community events and meetings
- Responds to constituent concerns
- Studies policy issues between sessions
- **Time Commitment:** 20-40 hours/week during session; 10-20 hours/week off-session
- **Meetings:** Daily during session (Jan-Apr); Committee meetings; Constituent meetings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Legislative power** - Pass laws (requires House concurrence)
- **Veto override** - Simple majority overrides Governor veto
- **Appointment confirmation** - Confirms Governor's appointments
- **Budget authority** - Must approve all state spending
- **Impeachment trial** - Tries impeachments (House initiates)
- Equal representation (50 senators, equal districts)

**Procedural Powers:**
- **Filibuster** - Can delay votes through extended debate (no formal limit in Indiana)
- Committee chairs control which bills get hearings
- Can amend bills significantly to change intent
- Pocket veto - Let bills die in committee
- Can add unrelated amendments to bills
- Majority party controls committee assignments and chairmanships
- Can force votes on politically difficult issues
- Leadership controls floor schedule and debate time

## Leadership
- **President:** Lieutenant Governor (constitutional)
- **President Pro Tempore:** Elected by Senate members
