# State Party Chair (Republican Party of Indiana)

## Overview
The State Party Chair is the highest-ranking officer of the Republican Party of Indiana, responsible for leading the party organization, coordinating campaigns, and representing the party publicly.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the State Committee
- Must be a registered Republican voter in Indiana
- No specific residency, age, or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by the State Committee (composed of precinct committeemen, district chairs, and other party officials)
- Typically elected at the State Convention or State Committee meeting
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of State Committee members present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Party Leadership**: Direct overall operations and strategy of the state Republican Party
- **Fundraising**: Lead fundraising efforts for state and local Republican candidates
- **Campaign Coordination**: Coordinate with candidates, county parties, and national GOP
- **Public Relations**: Serve as primary spokesperson for the state party
- **Staff Management**: Oversee party staff and day-to-day operations
- **Platform Development**: Guide development and promotion of party platform
- **State Committee Meetings**: Preside over State Committee meetings
- **Candidate Recruitment**: Work to recruit strong candidates for state and local offices

**Time Commitment**: Full-time position (typically compensated)
- Daily oversight of party operations
- Frequent travel across Indiana to meet with county parties
- Regular media appearances and public speaking engagements
- Extensive fundraising activities

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Appoint party staff and manage party headquarters
- Set agenda for State Committee meetings
- Control party messaging and communications
- Direct allocation of party resources (within budget constraints)
- Represent Indiana GOP at Republican National Committee meetings
- Execute party strategy for campaigns and voter outreach

### Limitations:
- Cannot unilaterally change party rules or platform (requires State Committee approval)
- Subject to oversight by State Committee
- Cannot dictate to elected officials how to vote or govern
- Budget and major expenditures typically require State Committee approval
- Cannot remove precinct committeemen or other elected party officials without cause

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
- Can be removed by State Committee vote
