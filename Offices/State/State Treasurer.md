# State Treasurer of Indiana

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen
- Resident of Indiana
- At least 18 years old (typical constitutional requirement)

## Election Process
- Elected statewide on partisan ballot
- Independent election
- Elected in even-numbered, non-presidential election years

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None specified
- **Next Election:** November 2028

## Current Office Holder
**[Daniel Elliott](../../Profiles/State/State%20Treasurer%20-%20Daniel%20Elliott.md)** (Republican)
- **Assumed Office:** January 9, 2023
- **Previous Experience:** Morgan County Council, software entrepreneur

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - State's chief financial officer
- Manages state cash flow and investments
- Administers unclaimed property program
- Manages CollegeChoice 529 savings program
- Chairs Indiana Bond Bank
- Serves on various state financial boards
- Issues state payments
- Banking oversight and regulation
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Board meetings, investment reviews, bank examinations, financial institution meetings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Investment authority** over state funds (billions of dollars)
- **Banking regulation** for state-chartered banks
- **Unclaimed property custody** and distribution
- **Board membership** on fiscal commissions
- Manages state's daily cash position

**Procedural Powers:**
- Investment decisions can favor certain institutions or strategies
- Can influence which banks hold state deposits (significant patronage)
- Timing of payments can affect cash flow for recipients
- Can advocate for changes to investment policies
- Discretion in promoting or changing 529 plan managers
- Platform for financial policy advocacy
- Can delay payments within legal bounds
- Control over lucrative financial services contracts
