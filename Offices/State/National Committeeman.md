# National Committeeman (Republican National Committee)

## Overview
The National Committeeman is Indiana's male representative to the Republican National Committee (RNC), representing Indiana Republicans at the national level and connecting state party operations with national GOP strategy.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by State Convention delegates or State Committee
- Must be a registered Republican voter in Indiana
- Must be male (counterpart to National Committeewoman)
- No specific residency, age, or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by delegates at the State Convention
- Elections typically held every four years
- Elected by majority vote of convention delegates
- Term coincides with presidential election cycle

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **RNC Representation**: Serve as one of Indiana's three voting members on the Republican National Committee (along with National Committeewoman and State Party Chair)
- **National-State Liaison**: Communicate national GOP priorities to Indiana and Indiana concerns to national party
- **Presidential Politics**: Participate in presidential nominating process and national convention planning
- **RNC Meetings**: Attend quarterly RNC meetings (typically in Washington, D.C. or other major cities)
- **Committee Service**: Serve on RNC committees and task forces
- **Fundraising**: Assist with coordinated fundraising between state and national party
- **Strategic Input**: Provide input on national party platform, rules, and strategy

**Time Commitment**: Part-time position with periodic intensive periods
- Quarterly RNC meetings (3-4 days each)
- State Convention attendance
- Conference calls and meetings with RNC leadership
- Presidential campaign years require significantly more time

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Vote on all RNC matters, including:
  - RNC Chair election
  - Party rules changes
  - Presidential nomination procedures
  - Budget and financial matters
  - Site selection for national conventions
- Participate in presidential nominating process at national convention
- Serve on RNC committees with policy influence
- Access to national party resources and data

### Limitations:
- One vote among 168 RNC members (3 from each state plus territories)
- Cannot dictate state party operations (that's the State Chair's role)
- National party decisions made by majority vote of full RNC
- Subject to state party rules and State Committee
- Cannot unilaterally commit Indiana GOP to national positions

### Rarely Used Powers:
- Technically can participate in efforts to remove RNC Chair through committee vote
- Can propose rule changes to RNC for consideration
- Has voice in selecting presidential debate participants and formats

## Term Length
- 4-year term (coincides with presidential election cycle)
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
- Term begins after election at State Convention and ends at next convention
