# State Party Treasurer (Republican Party of Indiana)

## Overview
The State Party Treasurer is an officer of the Republican Party of Indiana responsible for managing party finances, maintaining financial records, and ensuring compliance with campaign finance laws.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the State Committee
- Must be a registered Republican voter in Indiana
- Should have financial management experience
- No specific residency, age, or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by the State Committee
- Typically elected at the State Convention or State Committee meeting
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of State Committee members present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Financial Management**: Oversee all party finances and accounts
- **Fundraising Records**: Maintain records of all contributions and expenditures
- **Financial Reporting**: Prepare and file required campaign finance reports with state and federal authorities
- **Budget Management**: Work with party leadership to develop and manage party budget
- **Compliance**: Ensure compliance with state and federal campaign finance laws
- **Audit Cooperation**: Facilitate any required financial audits
- **Financial Statements**: Provide regular financial reports to State Committee

**Time Commitment**: Part-time to full-time (depending on party activity level)
- Ongoing financial record-keeping and reporting
- Coordination with party staff and vendors
- Quarterly and annual reporting requirements
- Attendance at State Committee meetings

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Authority over party bank accounts and financial transactions
- Approve expenditures within approved budget
- Sign checks and authorize payments
- Access to all financial records and accounts
- Vote on State Committee matters

### Limitations:
- Major expenditures typically require approval from Chair or State Committee
- Subject to oversight by State Committee and external auditors
- Must comply with state and federal campaign finance regulations
- Cannot use party funds for unauthorized purposes
- Subject to removal for financial mismanagement

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
