# Indiana House of Representatives

## Requirements
- U.S. citizen
- Resident of Indiana for at least 2 years
- Resident of the district at time of election
- At least 21 years old

## Election Process
- Elected by district on partisan ballot
- **Total Seats:** 100 representatives
- Districts redrawn every 10 years after census

## Term
- **Length:** 2 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** All seats up for election every 2 years

## Roles
- **Part-time position** - Citizen legislature
- Participates in legislative session (typically January-April)
- Reviews and debates legislation
- Serves on committees
- Constituent services year-round
- Attends district events and meetings
- Responds to constituent concerns
- Studies policy between sessions
- **Time Commitment:** 20-40 hours/week during session; 10-20 hours/week off-session
- **Meetings:** Daily during session; Committee meetings; District meetings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Legislative power** - Pass laws (requires Senate concurrence)
- **Veto override** - Simple majority overrides Governor veto
- **Revenue bills** - Must initiate all tax/revenue legislation (House exclusive)
- **Budget authority** - Must approve all state spending
- **Impeachment** - Sole power to impeach state officials (Senate tries)
- **Presidential election** - If Electoral College ties, House delegations choose President

**Procedural Powers:**
- Committee chairs control which bills get hearings (significant gatekeeping)
- Can amend bills to change intent
- Pocket veto - Kill bills in committee
- Can attach unrelated amendments ("riders") to bills
- Majority party controls all committees and scheduling
- Can force politically difficult votes
- Leadership controls debate time and floor recognition
- Can use budget process to pressure Senate on policy

## Leadership
- **Speaker:** Elected by House members
- Current Speaker: Todd Huston
