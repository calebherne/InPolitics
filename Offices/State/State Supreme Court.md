# Indiana Supreme Court

## Requirements
- Licensed attorney
- Admitted to practice law in Indiana
- U.S. citizen

## Selection Process
- **NOT elected** - Appointed through merit selection
- Indiana Judicial Nominating Commission nominates candidates
- [Governor](Governor.md) appoints from nominees
- Retention elections every 10 years after initial appointment

## Term
- **Initial Term:** Until first retention election
- **Retention:** 10-year terms if retained by voters
- **Mandatory Retirement:** Age 75

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Professional judiciary
- Hears oral arguments in select cases
- Reviews appellate briefs and records
- Writes or joins opinions
- Decides which cases to accept for review
- Administers Indiana court system
- Develops court rules and procedures
- Disciplines attorneys statewide
- Participates in judicial education
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Weekly conferences to decide cases; Oral arguments monthly; Administrative meetings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Final authority** on Indiana law and state constitution
- **Judicial review** - Can strike down laws as unconstitutional
- **Appellate jurisdiction** over all Indiana cases
- **Original jurisdiction** in specific cases (rare)
- **Attorney discipline** - Can disbar or suspend lawyers
- **Rule-making** - Creates rules governing courts and legal practice
- **Administrative control** over all Indiana courts

**Procedural Powers:**
- **Discretionary review** - Chooses which appeals to hear (sets legal precedent)
- Can craft constitutional interpretations that effectively create policy
- Stays of execution in death penalty cases
- Can declare legislative or executive actions unconstitutional
- Emergency orders affecting court operations statewide
- Controls court budgets and priorities through administrative powers
- Influential opinions can shape future legislation
- Can reinterpret precedent to change law without legislature
