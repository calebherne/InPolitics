# Indiana Tax Court

## Requirements
- Licensed attorney
- Admitted to practice law in Indiana
- U.S. citizen

## Selection Process
- **NOT elected** - Appointed through merit selection
- Indiana Judicial Nominating Commission nominates candidates
- [Governor](Governor.md) appoints from nominees
- Retention elections every 10 years after initial appointment

## Term
- **Initial Term:** Until first retention election
- **Retention:** 10-year terms if retained by voters
- **Mandatory Retirement:** Age 75

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Specialized tax judge
- Hears tax appeals and disputes
- Reviews property tax assessments
- Decides income tax cases
- Reviews decisions of Indiana Board of Tax Review
- Writes opinions on tax law
- Single judge handles all cases
- Participates in judicial education
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily hearings and oral arguments; Solo decision-making; Judicial conferences

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Exclusive jurisdiction** over state tax matters
- **Property tax appeals** - Final word on assessments (unless appealed to Supreme Court)
- **Income tax disputes**
- **Inheritance tax cases**
- **Tax interpretation** authority
- Single judge court (no panels)

**Procedural Powers:**
- Interpretations significantly affect property taxes statewide
- Can set precedents affecting billions in assessments
- Timing of decisions can affect tax years and collections
- Sole authority allows consistent interpretation (no conflicts)
- Can pressure settlements through preliminary rulings
- Opinions guide county assessors statewide
- Strategic case selection for published opinions
