# Hamilton County Sheriff

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County
- Typically requires law enforcement experience

## Election Process
- Elected county-wide
- Partisan election

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Chief law enforcement officer
- Manages sheriff's department (deputies, jail staff, support)
- Operates county jail and detention center
- Provides court security for all court sessions
- Serves warrants and civil process papers
- Patrols unincorporated areas of county
- Responds to emergencies
- Prepares and manages department budget
- Coordinates with local police departments
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-60+ hours/week, on-call 24/7
- **Meetings:** Daily briefings; County meetings; Law enforcement coordination; Community events

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Law enforcement authority** throughout entire county
- **Arrest powers** for any violation in county
- **Jail operation** - Custody of all county inmates
- **Civil process service** - Evictions, garnishments, subpoenas
- **Court security** - Protection of judges and courtrooms
- **Emergency response** authority
- **Hiring authority** for deputies and jail staff

**Procedural Powers:**
- Enforcement discretion (which laws to prioritize)
- Can decline to enforce certain laws (controversial but possible)
- Controls patrol coverage and presence in areas
- Jail conditions and treatment policies
- Can coordinate or refuse to coordinate with federal immigration
- Hiring creates political network and loyalty
- Platform to advocate for law enforcement policy
- Emergency declarations can expand authority temporarily
- Can influence local politics through law enforcement presence
