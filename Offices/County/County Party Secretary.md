# County Party Secretary (Republican Party)

## Overview
The County Party Secretary is an officer of the county Republican Party responsible for maintaining party records, documenting County Committee meetings, and ensuring proper administrative procedures.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the County Committee (composed of precinct committeemen)
- Must be a registered Republican voter in the county
- Must be a precinct committeeman (in most counties)
- No specific age or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by precinct committeemen at county caucus
- Typically elected at the same time as other county officers
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of precinct committeemen present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Record Keeping**: Maintain official records of County Committee meetings and proceedings
- **Meeting Minutes**: Record and distribute minutes of all County Committee meetings
- **Document Management**: Manage party documents, bylaws, and correspondence
- **Membership Records**: Maintain current list of precinct committeemen and their contact information
- **Notice Requirements**: Ensure proper notice is given for meetings per party rules
- **Credential Verification**: Verify credentials of precinct committeemen at caucuses and meetings

**Time Commitment**: Moderate volunteer commitment (typically unpaid)
- Attend all County Committee meetings
- Prepare and distribute meeting materials
- Maintain ongoing correspondence and record-keeping

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Custody of official county party records
- Certify official party documents and actions
- Vote on County Committee matters (if also a precinct committeeman)
- Access to party information as needed for duties

### Limitations:
- Administrative role with limited policy-making authority
- Subject to direction of County Party Chair and County Committee
- Cannot unilaterally alter official records
- Decisions on party matters require County Committee vote

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
