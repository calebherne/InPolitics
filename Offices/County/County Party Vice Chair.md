# County Party Vice Chair (Republican Party)

## Overview
The County Party Vice Chair is the second-highest-ranking officer of the county Republican Party, assisting the County Party Chair and serving in their absence.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the County Committee (composed of precinct committeemen)
- Must be a registered Republican voter in the county
- Must be a precinct committeeman (in most counties)
- No specific age or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by precinct committeemen at county caucus
- Typically elected at the same time as the County Party Chair
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of precinct committeemen present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Assist County Chair**: Support the County Party Chair in all party operations
- **Substitute Leadership**: Preside over County Committee meetings in the Chair's absence
- **Committee Coordination**: Often responsible for coordinating specific committees or initiatives
- **Precinct Support**: Work with precinct committeemen on voter outreach and organization
- **Event Support**: Assist with planning and executing county party events
- **Campaign Coordination**: Help coordinate campaign activities during elections

**Time Commitment**: Moderate volunteer commitment (typically unpaid)
- Attend County Committee meetings
- Fill in for Chair when needed
- Coordinate assigned responsibilities
- Increased activity during election years

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Exercise powers of the County Chair when Chair is absent or unable to serve
- Vote on County Committee matters (if also a precinct committeeman)
- Represent county party at events when needed
- Participate in county party leadership decisions

### Limitations:
- Authority generally subordinate to the County Party Chair
- Cannot override decisions of the Chair or County Committee
- Powers when substituting for Chair are typically temporary
- Subject to County Committee oversight

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
- Succession: Typically becomes acting Chair if Chair position becomes vacant until a new election
