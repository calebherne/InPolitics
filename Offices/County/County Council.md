# Hamilton County Council

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County
- For district seats: resident of the district

## Election Process
- Seven members total
- **4 elected by district:**
  - District 1: Parts of Clay Township (45 precincts)
  - District 2: Delaware, Fall Creek, Wayne Townships
  - District 3: Noblesville, Jackson, White River Townships
  - District 4: Parts of Clay Township (17 precincts), Adams, Washington Townships
- **3 elected at-large** (county-wide)
- Partisan elections

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Elections:** Varies

## Roles
- **Part-time position**
- Review and approve county budget annually
- Set salaries for county employees
- Approve special appropriations
- Consider local tax proposals
- Meet regularly (typically monthly)
- Review fiscal matters with Auditor and Treasurer
- Constituent services
- Study budget requests from departments
- **Time Commitment:** 10-20 hours/week (more during budget season)
- **Meetings:** Regular council meetings (monthly); Budget hearings (summer/fall); Committee meetings; Public hearings on taxes

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Fiscal control** - All county spending must be approved
- **Salary setting** - Sets pay for all county employees
- **Budget adoption** - Annual budget authority
- **Tax authority** - Can impose local income, excise, and service taxes (with limits)
- Income tax requires state approval
- Property tax indirectly through budget approval

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can defund county initiatives by budget cuts
- Salary control provides leverage over elected officials and departments
- Can add conditions to appropriations
- Tax increases are politically powerful tool
- Can force Commissioners to justify expenses
- Delay or deny emergency appropriations
- Platform to critique county management
- Can earmark funds for specific purposes
