# County Party Treasurer (Republican Party)

## Overview
The County Party Treasurer is an officer of the county Republican Party responsible for managing party finances, maintaining financial records, and ensuring compliance with campaign finance laws.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the County Committee (composed of precinct committeemen)
- Must be a registered Republican voter in the county
- Must be a precinct committeeman (in most counties)
- Should have financial management experience
- No specific age or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by precinct committeemen at county caucus
- Typically elected at the same time as other county officers
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of precinct committeemen present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **Financial Management**: Oversee all county party finances and accounts
- **Fundraising Records**: Maintain records of all contributions and expenditures
- **Financial Reporting**: Prepare and file required campaign finance reports with state authorities
- **Budget Management**: Work with County Chair to develop and manage county party budget
- **Compliance**: Ensure compliance with state and federal campaign finance laws
- **Disbursements**: Process payments for party expenses and activities
- **Financial Statements**: Provide regular financial reports to County Committee

**Time Commitment**: Moderate volunteer commitment (typically unpaid)
- Ongoing financial record-keeping and reporting
- Coordination with party leadership and vendors
- Quarterly reporting requirements
- Attendance at County Committee meetings

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Authority over county party bank accounts and financial transactions
- Approve expenditures within approved budget
- Sign checks and authorize payments (often requires co-signature of Chair)
- Vote on County Committee matters (if also a precinct committeeman)

### Limitations:
- Major expenditures typically require approval from Chair or County Committee
- Subject to oversight by County Committee
- Must comply with state and federal campaign finance regulations
- Cannot use party funds for unauthorized purposes
- Subject to removal for financial mismanagement

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
