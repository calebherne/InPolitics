# Hamilton County Treasurer

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County

## Election Process
- Elected county-wide
- Partisan election

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - County's money manager
- Collects all property tax payments
- Manages county cash flow and investments
- Maintains tax payment records
- Distributes tax revenue to taxing units:
  - Schools
  - Townships
  - Cities and towns
  - Special districts
- Processes tax refunds and adjustments
- Handles delinquent property tax sales
- Invests county funds for maximum return
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 40-50 hours/week (more during tax season)
- **Meetings:** Regular with staff; County Council; Auditor coordination; Investment committee; Taxing unit distributions

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Tax collection authority** for all property taxes
- **Investment authority** for county funds
- **Delinquent tax procedures** - Penalties and tax sales
- **Distribution authority** - Sends money to all taxing units
- **Cash management** for county operations

**Procedural Powers:**
- Investment choices affect county income (and which banks/firms benefit)
- Can be aggressive or lenient on delinquent taxpayers
- Controls timing of distributions to taxing units
- Can highlight or downplay tax collection issues
- Platform to advocate for tax policy changes
- Works with Auditor (cooperation or conflict affects efficiency)
- Media attention during tax season provides visibility
- Banking relationships can involve political patronage
- Can expedite or delay tax refunds
- Tax sale procedures affect property owners losing homes
