# Hamilton County Commissioners

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County
- Resident of their respective district

## Election Process
- Three commissioners elected by district
- **District 1:** Carmel/Clay Township
- **District 2:** Fishers, Noblesville, Delaware Township, Noblesville Township
- **District 3:** Adams, Fall Creek, Jackson, Washington, Wayne, White River Townships, plus Arcadia, Atlanta, Cicero, Sheridan, Westfield
- Partisan elections

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Terms Staggered:** Yes
- **Next Elections:** Varies by district

## Roles
- **Part-time position** (though requires significant commitment)
- Executive management of county operations
- Attend regular commission meetings (typically weekly or bi-weekly)
- Review and approve contracts
- Appoint board and commission members
- Respond to constituent concerns
- Oversee county department heads
- Coordinate with County Council on budget
- **Time Commitment:** 20-30 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Regular commissioner meetings (public); Department head meetings; Board appointments; Public events

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Executive authority** - Carry out county legislation
- **Contract execution** - Sign agreements on behalf of county
- **Appointment power** - Name members to boards and commissions
- **Department oversight** - Manage county employees and operations
- **One commissioner serves as president** (typically by seniority)

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can influence which contracts are pursued or prioritized
- Appointment power creates political network and patronage
- Control county resources and priorities
- Can delay or expedite county projects
- Influence hiring of department heads
- Bully pulpit to shape county priorities
- President role provides tie-breaking and agenda control
- Can coordinate with Council members on budget strategy
