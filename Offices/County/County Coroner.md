# Hamilton County Coroner

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County

## Election Process
- Elected county-wide
- Partisan election

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Part-time to full-time** depending on county workload
- Investigates deaths requiring official inquiry
- Determines cause and manner of death
- Orders and reviews autopsies
- Works with law enforcement on suspicious deaths
- Issues death certificates for cases under jurisdiction
- Maintains death investigation records
- Testifies in court when needed
- Manages coroner's office staff
- **Time Commitment:** 20-40 hours/week, on-call 24/7
- **Meetings:** Death scene investigations; Autopsy attendance; Court testimony; Family notifications

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Death investigation authority** for:
  - Sudden/unexplained deaths
  - Deaths without medical attendance
  - Suspicious or violent deaths
  - Deaths in custody
- **Autopsy orders** - Can compel examination
- **Custody of body** during investigation
- **Subpoena power** for death investigations
- **Death certification** for cases under jurisdiction

**Procedural Powers:**
- Determination of "manner of death" can be politically sensitive (suicide vs. accident, etc.)
- Can request law enforcement investigations
- Controls narrative around controversial deaths
- Autopsy decisions affect families and can reveal information
- Platform to advocate for public health issues (drug deaths, etc.)
- Can call coroner's inquest (jury investigation) in controversial cases
- Media attention on high-profile cases provides visibility
