# Township Trustee

## Requirements
- Resident of the township
- Must be elected by township voters

## Election Process
- Elected by township voters
- Partisan election
- **Hamilton County has 9 townships:**
  - Adams Township
  - Clay Township
  - Delaware Township
  - Fall Creek Township
  - Jackson Township
  - Noblesville Township
  - Washington Township (Westfield-Washington)
  - Wayne Township
  - White River Township

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Elections:** Varies by township

## Roles
- **Part-time to full-time** depending on township size
- Administers civil government of township
- Provides fire protection and ambulance service to unincorporated areas
- Administers poor relief and burial of indigent
- Maintains cemeteries and burial grounds
- Resolves fencing disputes between neighbors
- Investigates livestock claims
- Weed control enforcement
- Manages township budget and financial records
- Serves as property tax assessor
- Annual financial reporting
- Manages township assistance programs
- May be involved in:
  - Zoning
  - Parks
  - Libraries
  - Community centers
- **Time Commitment:** 20-40 hours/week depending on township
- **Meetings:** Regular office hours; Monthly board meetings; Emergency assistance consultations; Fire/EMS coordination

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Poor relief authority** - Provides emergency assistance
- **Fire and ambulance services** - Essential services in unincorporated areas
- **Cemetery management** responsibility
- **Property tax assessment** authority
- **Township budget** execution
- **Dispute resolution** - Fencing, livestock claims
- **Hiring authority** for township employees

**Procedural Powers:**
- Discretion in providing poor relief (who gets help, how much)
- Fire and EMS decisions affect safety and response times
- Can be generous or stingy with assistance
- Assessment decisions affect property taxes
- Controls township spending priorities
- Platform to advocate for local issues
- Works with or against [Township Board](Township%20Board.md)
- Emergency assistance can build political support
- Dispute resolution builds community relationships
- Zoning involvement affects development (where applicable)
