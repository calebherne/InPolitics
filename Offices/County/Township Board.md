# Township Board

## Requirements
- Resident of the township
- Must be elected by township voters

## Election Process
- Three members elected per township
- Elected by township voters
- Partisan election
- **Hamilton County has 9 townships:**
  - Adams Township
  - Clay Township
  - Delaware Township
  - Fall Creek Township
  - Jackson Township
  - Noblesville Township
  - Washington Township (Westfield-Washington)
  - Wayne Township
  - White River Township

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Elections:** Varies by township

## Roles
- **Part-time position** - Legislative body for township
- Assists [Township Trustee](Township%20Trustee.md) in township governance
- Adopts annual budget
- Serves as board of finance
- Approves township contracts
- Reviews trustee's annual report showing:
  - Receipts
  - Expenditures
  - Investments
  - Debts
- Report published in local papers for public inspection
- **Time Commitment:** 5-15 hours/week, more during budget season
- **Meetings:** Monthly meetings; Budget hearings; Special meetings as needed; Public budget review

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Budget approval** - Must adopt annual budget
- **Board of finance** authority
- **Contract approval** for township
- **Oversight** of trustee operations
- **Financial review** - Examine trustee's reports
- **Public accountability** - Reports published for public

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can approve or deny trustee's budget requests
- Budget control affects township services
- Can investigate trustee spending irregularities
- Platform to criticize or support trustee
- Collective action can force trustee policy changes
- Can delay approvals to pressure trustee
- Poor relief policy recommendations affect residents
- Contract approvals can expedite or delay projects
- Public reports can embarrass or vindicate trustee
