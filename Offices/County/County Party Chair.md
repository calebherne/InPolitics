# County Party Chair (Republican Party)

## Overview
The County Party Chair is the highest-ranking officer of the Republican Party within a county, responsible for leading the local party organization, coordinating campaigns, and representing the party at the county level.

## Requirements
- Must be elected by the County Committee (composed of precinct committeemen)
- Must be a registered Republican voter in the county
- Must be a precinct committeeman (in most counties)
- No specific age or citizenship requirements beyond being a voter

## How Elected
- Elected by precinct committeemen at a county caucus
- Typically held shortly after primary election (or after precinct committeemen are elected/take office)
- Elections held every two years (even-numbered years)
- Elected by majority vote of precinct committeemen present

## Roles and Responsibilities
- **County Party Leadership**: Direct overall operations and strategy of the county Republican Party
- **Campaign Coordination**: Coordinate Republican campaigns for local, state, and federal offices
- **Fundraising**: Lead fundraising efforts for county party and local candidates
- **Voter Outreach**: Organize voter registration drives, get-out-the-vote efforts, and precinct operations
- **County Committee Meetings**: Preside over County Committee meetings
- **State Committee Member**: Serve as voting member of State Committee
- **Precinct Organization**: Work with precinct committeemen to build strong precinct-level organization
- **Public Relations**: Serve as spokesperson for county Republican Party
- **Event Coordination**: Organize county party events, Lincoln Day dinners, and candidate forums
- **Candidate Recruitment**: Recruit Republican candidates for local offices

**Time Commitment**: Significant volunteer commitment (typically unpaid, except in very large counties)
- Regular County Committee meetings (monthly or quarterly)
- State Committee meetings (quarterly)
- Ongoing coordination with candidates and elected officials
- Significantly increased time during election years
- Event planning and attendance

## Powers and Limitations
### Powers:
- Preside over County Committee meetings
- Vote on State Committee matters
- Set agenda for county party activities
- Appoint committee chairs and coordinators (with County Committee approval)
- Control county party messaging and communications
- Direct allocation of county party resources
- Represent county at state party meetings and events
- Nominate candidates to fill vacancies in some local offices (per state law)

### Limitations:
- Subject to oversight by County Committee (precinct committeemen)
- Cannot remove precinct committeemen (they are elected by voters)
- Major expenditures typically require County Committee approval
- Cannot dictate to elected officials how to vote or govern
- Subject to state party rules and bylaws
- Can be removed by majority vote of County Committee

## Term Length
- 2-year term
- No term limits
- Can be re-elected indefinitely
- Term begins after election at county caucus and ends at next caucus
