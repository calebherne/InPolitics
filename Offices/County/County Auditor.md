# Hamilton County Auditor

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County

## Election Process
- Elected county-wide
- Partisan election

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - County's chief fiscal officer
- Maintains all county financial records
- Processes and tracks county payments
- Issues paychecks to county employees
- Maintains property tax records and ownership data
- Calculates property tax bills with Treasurer
- Audits county accounts and transactions
- Prepares financial reports for state
- Serves on various county boards
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 40-50 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily with staff; County Council budget meetings; Board meetings; Commissioner meetings; Public tax hearings

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Fiscal control** - All payments must go through Auditor
- **Property tax administration** - Maintains tax records
- **Audit authority** over county transactions
- **Official county books** - Keeper of financial records
- **Board membership** on fiscal bodies

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can delay payments by questioning legality or appropriateness
- Pre-audit authority provides oversight of spending
- Controls financial information and reporting
- Can highlight fiscal irregularities publicly
- Platform to advocate for financial transparency
- Property tax calculations affect thousands of taxpayers
- Works closely with Treasurer (can coordinate or conflict)
- Can refer suspected fraud for investigation
- Financial reports can embarrass or vindicate officials
