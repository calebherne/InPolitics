# Hamilton County Surveyor

## Requirements
- Must declare party affiliation
- Resident of Hamilton County
- Licensed professional surveyor (typically required)

## Election Process
- Elected county-wide
- Partisan election

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Part-time position** in most counties
- Maintains county survey records
- Establishes and maintains property boundaries
- Maintains section corners and benchmarks
- Oversees county drainage systems
- Reviews subdivision plats
- Provides surveying services to county
- Reviews engineering plans affecting drainage
- Drainage board responsibilities
- Resolves property boundary disputes
- **Time Commitment:** Part-time, 10-20 hours/week (more for large projects)
- **Meetings:** As needed; Drainage board meetings; Subdivision reviews; Boundary dispute consultations

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Official surveyor** for county
- **Drainage authority** - Manages county drains
- **Plat approval** for subdivisions
- **Boundary determination** authority
- **Survey records** maintenance
- Member of County Drainage Board

**Procedural Powers:**
- Subdivision approval can expedite or delay development
- Drainage decisions affect property development and flooding
- Survey interpretations can resolve disputes
- Can recommend drainage projects (affecting property owners)
- Platform to advocate for drainage and development issues
- Technical expertise gives authority in disputes
- Drainage assessments affect property owners' costs
- Works with developers (relationships matter)
- Engineering plan reviews can influence projects
