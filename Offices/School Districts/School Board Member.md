# School Board Member

## Overview
School boards in Indiana are elected to govern local school corporations/districts. Hamilton County has 6 school districts, each with an elected school board.

## Requirements
- Resident of the school district
- At least 18 years old
- U.S. citizen
- Must file petition with required signatures

## Election Process
- Elected by school district (not city/county-wide)
- **Nonpartisan elections** (no party affiliation required)
- Board size varies by district (typically 5-7 members)
- May be elected by district or at-large depending on district

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** Held in November of even-numbered years
- **Next Election:** November 2026

## Roles
- **Part-time volunteer position** (unpaid or minimal stipend in most districts)
- Set policies for school district
- Hire and evaluate superintendent
- Adopt annual budget (often $100+ million)
- Set property tax levy for schools (subject to state caps)
- Approve curriculum and programs
- Negotiate teacher contracts
- Approve construction projects and facilities
- Ensure compliance with state and federal laws
- Review student discipline policies
- Approve contracts with vendors
- Most school funding comes from property taxes
- **Time Commitment:** 10-20 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Monthly board meetings (2-4 hours); Committee meetings; Budget hearings; Community forums; Superintendent evaluations; Labor negotiations

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Superintendent hiring/firing** - Hires chief executive
- **Budget adoption** - Controls spending (often $100M+)
- **Tax levy authority** - Sets property tax rate (within state caps)
- **Policy creation** - All district policies
- **Contract approval** - Teacher contracts, vendor contracts
- **Curriculum approval** - What students learn
- **Construction authority** - Facilities and bond issues

**Procedural Powers:**
- Superintendent evaluation affects district direction
- Budget priorities determine programs (what gets funded)
- Can cut or expand programs through budget
- Curriculum decisions can be controversial (books, topics)
- Teacher contract negotiations affect compensation
- Construction decisions affect taxpayers (referendums)
- Platform to advocate for education issues
- Can investigate district problems
- Hiring superintendent creates policy alignment
- Nonpartisan but often ideological battles
- Parental concerns create media attention
- Can pressure superintendent through evaluation
