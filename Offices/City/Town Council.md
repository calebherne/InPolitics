# Town Council

## Overview
Smaller communities in Indiana (like Sheridan, Cicero, Arcadia, Atlanta in Hamilton County) are incorporated as **towns** rather than cities. Towns have a slightly different structure.

## Requirements
- Resident of the town
- At least 18 years old
- U.S. citizen
- Must declare party affiliation

## Election Process
- **Council size:** 3, 5, or 7 members
- Elected town-wide or by district
- Partisan elections (unlike some states, Indiana requires party affiliation)

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** Varies by town
- **Elections:** Held in odd-numbered years (municipal elections)

## Roles
- **Part-time position** - Serves both legislative AND executive functions
- No separate mayor position (different from cities)
- **Council President** elected by fellow members:
  - Serves as town's executive officer
  - Leads council meetings
  - Represents town officially
  - Signs contracts and documents
- May appoint **Town Manager** (optional):
  - Non-partisan professional administrator
  - Handles day-to-day operations
- Passes town ordinances
- Adopts budget and sets tax rates
- Hires town employees (police, fire, public works)
- Oversees town departments
- Manages town services
- Unlike cities, combines mayor and council roles
- **Time Commitment:** Part-time, 15-25 hours/week
- **Meetings:** Bi-weekly or monthly council meetings; Committee meetings; Public hearings; Department coordination

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Combined legislative and executive authority**
- **Ordinance passage** - Legislative power
- **Department management** - Executive power
- **Budget adoption** and tax rate setting
- **Hiring and firing** town employees
- **Contract execution** through Council President
- **Policy creation** for town operations

**Procedural Powers:**
- Council President has enhanced influence as executive
- Majority control means unified government (no mayor vs. council)
- Can act quickly without executive/legislative division
- Hiring creates political loyalty
- Town Manager appointment (if used) creates professional buffer
- Zoning and development decisions affect growth
- Platform for higher office
- Small town visibility provides name recognition
- Can prioritize certain areas/residents through services
- Budget control without veto possibility
