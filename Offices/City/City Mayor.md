# City Mayor

## Overview
Hamilton County cities (Carmel, Fishers, Noblesville, Westfield) use a **mayor-council** form of government.

## Requirements
- Resident of the city
- At least 18 years old
- U.S. citizen

## Election Process
- Elected city-wide
- Partisan elections (in most Indiana cities)
- Each city elects its own mayor

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** Varies by city
- **Elections:** Held in odd-numbered years (municipal elections)

## Roles
- **Full-time position** - Chief executive of city government
- Oversees all city departments
- Proposes city budget to [City Council](City%20Council.md)
- Appoints department heads (police, fire, public works, etc.)
- Signs or vetoes ordinances passed by council
- Represents city in official functions
- Manages day-to-day city operations
- Sets city priorities and initiatives
- Responds to emergencies
- Works with businesses for economic development
- **Time Commitment:** Full-time, 50-70+ hours/week
- **Meetings:** Daily staff meetings; Weekly cabinet meetings; Council meetings; Community events; Business recruitment

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Executive authority** over city departments
- **Veto power** over council ordinances
- **Appointment power** for department heads
- **Budget proposal** authority
- **Emergency powers** during crises
- **Contract execution** on behalf of city
- **Ceremonial authority** as city representative

**Procedural Powers:**
- Controls city government operations and priorities
- Can reorganize departments within legal limits
- Hiring creates political network and loyalty
- Veto threat can shape legislation
- Platform for higher office (many state/federal officials start as mayors)
- Economic development decisions favor certain developers/businesses
- Emergency declarations can expand temporary authority
- Can propose or oppose tax increases
- Media attention provides bully pulpit for initiatives
- Controls pace and focus of city projects
