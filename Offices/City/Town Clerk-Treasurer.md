# Town Clerk-Treasurer

## Overview
Indiana towns elect a **Clerk-Treasurer** who handles financial and administrative functions independently of the [Town Council](Town%20Council.md).

## Requirements
- Resident of the town
- At least 18 years old
- U.S. citizen
- Must declare party affiliation

## Election Process
- Elected town-wide
- Partisan election
- Independent elected official (not appointed by council)

## Term
- **Length:** 4 years
- **Term Limits:** None
- **Elections:** Held in odd-numbered years (municipal elections)

## Roles
- **Full-time position** (in larger towns) or **Part-time** (in smaller towns)
- Independent elected official (not appointed by council)
- Manages town finances and accounting
- Keeps all financial records
- Pays town bills (within approved budget)
- Collects town revenues
- Prepares financial reports for council and state
- Clerk duties:
  - Maintains town records
  - Records council meeting minutes
  - Custodian of official town documents
- Operates independently but within budget approved by [Town Council](Town%20Council.md)
- **Time Commitment:** 30-40 hours/week (varies by town size)
- **Meetings:** Attends all council meetings; Daily office hours; Financial reporting; Public records requests

## Powers
**Constitutional Powers:**
- **Independent fiscal authority** - Elected separately from council
- **Financial management** within approved budget
- **Bill payment** authority
- **Revenue collection** responsibility
- **Official record keeper** for town
- **Meeting minutes** maintenance
- **Public records** custodian

**Procedural Powers:**
- Can question expenditure legality within budget
- Controls payment timing and cash flow
- Financial reporting can highlight issues or irregularities
- Platform to advocate for fiscal responsibility
- Works with or against Council (independence matters)
- Can delay payments if questions arise
- Access to all financial information provides oversight
- Can identify wasteful spending publicly
- Records preservation affects transparency
