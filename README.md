# Indiana & Hamilton County Politics Wiki

A concise reference wiki for political positions in Indiana state government and Hamilton County, Indiana.

## Folder Structure

This repository is organized into two main directories:

### üìÅ **Offices/** 
Contains information about government positions (roles, powers, requirements, election process, term lengths). Organized by level:
- `Offices/State/` - Indiana state-level positions
- `Offices/County/` - Hamilton County positions
- `Offices/City/` - Municipal government positions
- `Offices/School Districts/` - School board information
- `Offices/Federal/` - U.S. Congress positions

### üìÅ **Profiles/**
Contains biographical profiles of current office holders (party, tenure, background, immigration stance, religion, accomplishments, controversies). Mirrors the Offices/ structure:
- `Profiles/State/` - State official profiles
- `Profiles/County/` - County official profiles
- `Profiles/City/` - City official profiles
- `Profiles/School Districts/` - School board member profiles
- `Profiles/Federal/` - Federal delegation profiles

Each **Office file** links to current holder's profile (`../../Profiles/[category]/[Name].md`).  
Each **Profile file** links back to the office (`../../Offices/[category]/[Position].md`).

## Election Cycles

- **Federal Positions:** November of even-numbered years (2026, 2028, etc.)
  - U.S. Representatives: every 2 years
  - U.S. Senators: every 6 years (staggered)
- **State Executive & Legislature:** November of even-numbered, non-presidential years (2026, 2030, etc.)
- **County Positions:** November of even-numbered years (2026, 2028, etc.)
- **School Boards:** November of even-numbered years (2026, 2028, etc.) - Nonpartisan
- **Municipal Positions:** November of odd-numbered years (2027, 2031, etc.)

## Key Facts

### Hamilton County
- **County Seat:** Noblesville
- **Population:** 347,467 (2020)
- **Largest City:** Fishers
- **Townships:** 9 total
- **Congressional District:** 5th
- **Political Lean:** Republican stronghold, trending more competitive

### Indiana State
- **Capital:** Indianapolis
- **General Assembly:** Bicameral (Senate + House)
- **Senate:** 50 members, 4-year terms (staggered)
- **House:** 100 members, 2-year terms
- **Current Governor:** Mike Braun (R) - 2025

## Notes

- All elected positions require U.S. citizenship and residency requirements
- Most county and state positions require party affiliation declaration
- Judges are appointed through merit selection with retention elections
- Term limits vary by position
- Indiana follows Eastern Time Zone
