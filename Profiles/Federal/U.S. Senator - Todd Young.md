## Political Career Timeline
- **2000-2010:** Legislative assistant and advisor positions
- **2011-2017:** U.S. Representative (Indiana 9th District)
  - Member of Ways and Means Committee
  - Member of Budget Committee
- **2017-present:** U.S. Senator from Indiana (senior senator since 2025)
  - Re-elected 2022 with 58.51% of vote
  - Defeated Democrat Evan Bayh in 2016 (54.4% to 42.4%)

## What Known For

### Legislative Achievements & Focus

**Foreign Policy & National Security:**
- Strong focus on U.S.-China competition and national security
- Advocate for strengthening alliances and international engagement
- Marine Corps veteran with continued interest in defense policy
- Serves on Senate Foreign Relations Committee

**Bipartisan Efforts:**
- Known for working across aisle on selected issues
- More moderate than many Indiana Republicans
- Willing to break with party on certain votes

**CHIPS Act (2022):**
- Co-sponsored Creating Helpful Incentives to Produce Semiconductors (CHIPS) Act
- Signed into law to boost domestic semiconductor manufacturing
- Major bipartisan industrial policy achievement

**Endless Frontier Act:**
- Key proponent of legislation to increase U.S. investment in technology and research
- Aimed at competing with China's technological advances
- Provisions incorporated into broader legislation

**Indiana Economic Development:**
- Focuses on bringing federal investment to Indiana
- Advocates for manufacturing and infrastructure projects
- Works with both parties on state-specific priorities

### Senate Leadership & Committees

**Committee Assignments (recent):**
- Foreign Relations Committee
- Finance Committee
- Commerce, Science, and Transportation Committee
- Small Business and Entrepreneurship Committee

**Leadership Roles:**
- Has held various committee leadership positions
- Respected voice on foreign policy within GOP

### Notable Positions & Votes

**Trump Relationship:**
- More independent relationship with Donald Trump than many GOP senators
- Voted to certify 2020 election results
- Did not object to electoral college certification on January 6, 2021
- Sometimes breaks with Trump wing of party

**COVID-19 Response:**
- Supported some COVID-19 relief measures
- Balanced approach between public health and economic concerns

**Supreme Court Nominations:**
- Voted to confirm Trump's Supreme Court nominees
- Supported Barrett, Kavanaugh, Gorsuch confirmations

**Ukraine Support:**
- Has generally supported aid to Ukraine following Russian invasion
- Advocates for strong U.S. support of allies facing aggression

### 2022 Re-election Campaign
- Won with 58.51% of vote against Democrat Thomas McDermott Jr.
- Strong performance across Indiana
- Demonstrated durability in Republican-leaning state
