## Political Career Timeline
- **2000:** Immigrated to United States from Ukraine
- **2006:** Became U.S. citizen
- **Founding member:** Hamilton County Tea Party
- **2017-2020:** Indiana State Senator (20th district) - appointed after Luke Kenley resigned
- **2021-present:** U.S. Representative (Indiana 5th District)
  - **First Ukrainian-born person to serve in U.S. Congress**

## Immigration Stance
**Complex - Personal immigrant story but no clear policy positions documented**

Despite being a **first-generation immigrant from Ukraine** who came to U.S. in 2000 and became citizen in 2006, Spartz has **not taken prominent public positions on immigration policy**. Her personal narrative emphasizes legal immigration and opportunity.

**Personal Story:**
- Immigrated legally from post-Soviet Ukraine at age 22
- Met American husband Jason on train; married 2000
- Emphasizes "American Dream still exists" through legal immigration path

**Policy Positions:**
- No documented voting record or statements on comprehensive immigration reform
- No clear position on border security, DREAM Act, or other major immigration issues
- Serves on House Judiciary Committee's Subcommittee on Immigration Integrity, Security, and Enforcement (118th Congress)

**Note:** Unusual that first-generation immigrant in Congress has not articulated clear immigration policy positions. This may reflect:
- Focus on other policy priorities (Ukraine, healthcare, fiscal issues)
- Desire to avoid politically divisive issue
- Tea Party conservative base in Hamilton County expects restrictionist positions

## What Known For

### Russian Invasion of Ukraine (2022-2024)

**Initial Strong Support:**
- Called Russian invasion **"a genocide of the Ukrainian people by a crazy man"**
- **First U.S. officials to visit Ukraine** after invasion (with Sen. Steve Daines to Bucha, April 2022)
- Second trip to Lviv, Kyiv, and Odesa with Rep. Tim Walberg
- Family in Ukraine including grandmother in besieged Chernihiv
- Hometown Chernihiv initially lauded her as "one of their own"

**Criticism of Zelenskyy (July 2022):**
- Accused Ukrainian President Zelenskyy of **"playing politics and theater"** and not governing seriously
- Made six allegations against Andrii Yermak (top Zelenskyy aide):
  - Leaking secret information to Belarus and Russia
  - Mismanaging peace negotiations with Russia
  - Falsely assuring no attack would happen (contrary to Western intelligence)
  - Delaying anti-corruption prosecutor appointment
- Requested White House investigate allegations
- Ukraine Foreign Ministry called accusations **"baseless speculation"**
- Former U.S. Ambassador Michael McFaul defended Yermak: "most certainly does not strike me as being pro-Russian"
- Republican lawmakers concerned accusations could hurt war effort and damage U.S.-Ukraine relations

**Vote Against Ukraine Aid (April 2024):**
- Voted **against crucial $60 billion aid package** for Ukraine
- Vote came 3 days after Russian missile strike on Chernihiv (her hometown) killed 18 civilians, injured 78
- Primary challenger had accused her of prioritizing Ukraine aid over domestic priorities like border wall
- Hometown reaction: Pride turned to **"anger"** and **"sense of betrayal"**
- Some Chernihiv residents felt abandoned by someone they considered their representative

**Sources:**

- [AP News: Spartz votes against aid for homeland](https://apnews.com/article/indiana-primary-election-victoria-spartz-ukraine-57fb403caa6a9bbe459639baacfdb7fa)

### Staff Turnover & Toxic Workplace Allegations

**Highest Staff Turnover in Congress (2021):**
- LegiStorm measured her 2021 turnover at **3.5 times the average** for House members
- **Highest turnover for non-retiring member**
- Last year's staff turnover was House's worst in decades

**Politico Investigation (May 2022):**
- Reported **"toxic" work environment**
- Spartz's temper described as "quickly jumping from tepid to boiling"
- Ordered staff to record her directions, then denied instructions despite recordings
- Former aide quote: "Common theme: Staffers do their job, and then Victoria comes in saying they have no idea what they're doing, that they are 'morons,' calling them 'idiots'"
- Spartz responded her working style is **"not for everyone"** and critics need to **"toughen up"**

**Ethics Committee Probe (June 2024):**
- House Ethics Committee opened investigation into allegations of **"abuse," "general toxicity," and "rage"** toward staff
- Probe delayed until after May 7, 2024 primary to avoid "potential appearance of meddling in election"
- Same month: Chief of staff quit after less than 1 month; communications director quit after less than 5 months

### Retirement Flip-Flop (2023-2024)
- **February 2023:** Announced she would **not run for office in 2024**
- **December 2023:** Walked back retirement, said "colleagues and constituents want me to run again" and there's "better Speaker" now
- **February 2024:** Confirmed re-election run (5 days before filing deadline)
- **May 2024:** Won GOP primary despite being outspent by main challenger Chuck Goodrich
- **November 2024:** Re-elected with 56.6% of vote

### Policy Positions

**Fiscal Hawk:**
- October 2023: Said she would **resign unless commission to manage national debt created by end of 2024**
- Co-sponsored Fiscal Commission Act of 2023
- **2025:** Initially opposed GOP budget resolution that would balloon deficit by trillions
- **Switched position after President Trump reportedly pressured her**

**Healthcare - Anticompetitive Focus:**
- 2022: Released bills to crack down on healthcare costs through curbing anti-competitive conduct
- Introduced legislation empowering FTC to rein in hospital mergers
- With Rep. Pramila Jayapal: Introduced Stop Anticompetitive Healthcare Act (December 2022)
- Op-ed: Argued hospital monopolies harming healthcare

**Socialism Opposition:**
- Born in Soviet Ukraine; criticizes socialism resurgence in U.S.
- Indianapolis Star: Upbringing "formed her belief government involvement is inherently bad and ineffective"
- Founding member of "Freedom Force" (GOP response to "The Squad")

**Israel Support:**
- Visited Israel September 2023 with bipartisan delegation
- Released statement supporting Israel after October 2023 Hamas attacks

**Agricultural Issues:**
- 2023: Introduced bill prohibiting federal funds in 2024 Agriculture bill from being used by commodity checkoff programs
- Agricultural industry groups objected (federal funds aren't used for checkoffs)
- Indicated support for OFF Act (more oversight of checkoff programs)
