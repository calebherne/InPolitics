# Attorney General - Todd Rokita

## Basic Information
- **Name**: Todd Rokita
- **Party**: Republican
- **Office**: Attorney General of Indiana
- **Assumed Office**: January 11, 2021
- **Age**: 56 (born February 9, 1970)

## Background
- **Birthplace**: Chicago, Illinois (raised in [Munster, Indiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Munster,_Indiana))
- **Nationality**: American
- **Generations in US**: At least 2nd generation (Polish descent)
- **Religion**: [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church) (member of Knights of Columbus)
- **Education**:
  - [Wabash College](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wabash_College) (BA)
  - [Indiana University Robert H. McKinney School of Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indiana_University_Robert_H._McKinney_School_of_Law) (JD)
- **Family**: Married to Kathy Rokita; 2 children (oldest son Teddy has [Angelman syndrome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angelman_syndrome))
- **Residence**: [Brownsburg, Indiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brownsburg,_Indiana)

## Political Career Timeline
- **2000**: Legal counsel for George W. Bush campaign Florida recount
- **2002-2010**: Indiana Secretary of State (youngest in US at age 32)
- **2011-2019**: U.S. Representative, [Indiana's 4th congressional district](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indiana%27s_4th_congressional_district)
- **2018**: Lost Republican primary for U.S. Senate to Mike Braun
- **2020**: Defeated incumbent Curtis Hill for Republican nomination for Attorney General
- **2021-present**: Attorney General of Indiana
- **2024**: Re-elected with 58.83% of vote

## Immigration Stance
**Hardline restrictionist**. Rokita has taken strong anti-immigration positions:
- **2017**: Introduced the [SLAP Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanctuary_city) (Stopping Lawless Actions of Politicians) to impose fines and jail time on state/local politicians implementing sanctuary city policies. [The bill did not pass](https://www.govtrack.us/congress/bills/115/hr4915).
- **2017**: Supported President Trump's [executive order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Order_13990) temporarily banning citizens of seven Muslim-majority countries from entering the U.S.
- Consistent advocate for strict border enforcement and opposition to sanctuary policies.

## What He's Known For

### As Attorney General:
- **[Abortion case controversy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Ohio_child-rape_and_Indiana_abortion_case)** (2022-2023): Investigated [Dr. Caitlin Bernard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caitlin_Bernard) after she provided abortion care to 10-year-old rape victim. Judge ruled Rokita ["clearly violated Indiana law"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Abortion_case) by discussing confidential investigation publicly. [Indiana Supreme Court reprimanded him and fined him $250](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Abortion_case) for attorney misconduct. Rokita claimed he "was not fined" and repeated his "abortion activist" description of Bernard.

- **Election fraud claims**: Endorsed [Texas v. Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Texas_v._Pennsylvania) lawsuit to challenge 2020 election results (Supreme Court rejected). [Declined to sign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Efforts_to_overturn_the_2020_presidential_election) National Association of Attorneys General letter condemning January 6 Capitol attack. February 2021 tweet [implying election was "stolen"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Efforts_to_overturn_the_2020_presidential_election) from Trump was flagged by Twitter.

- **"Eyes on Education" portal** (2024): Launched [website for reporting school materials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Eyes_on_Education) that allegedly violate Indiana law. School officials criticized lack of vetting before complaints appear publicly.

- **COVID-19 positions**: Filed multiple lawsuits against Biden administration vaccine mandates. Issued opinion that Indiana University's vaccine requirement violated state law.

### Earlier Career Controversies:
- **2007**: Made ["master and slave" comment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Controversial_comments) about African American voters at Republican event. Apologized to Indiana Black Legislative Caucus.

- **2013**: Told CNN journalist Carol Costello ["you're beautiful but you have to be honest"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#U.S._House_of_Representatives) during government shutdown interview, drawing criticism.

- **Trump alignment**: Voted with President Trump [90.3% of the time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Donald_Trump) during his time in Congress.

- **Healthcare industry payments**: Faced [2021 scrutiny](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Todd_Rokita#Attorney_General_of_Indiana) for collecting tens of thousands in payments from healthcare and pharmaceutical companies while in office.

### Notable Activities:
- Commercial-rated pilot who has flown veterans for free
- Strong advocate for voter ID laws (defended Indiana's law before U.S. Supreme Court)
- Proposed redistricting reform as Secretary of State (2009)
- Filed lawsuit against TikTok (2022) over data privacy concerns
- Multiple lawsuits against Biden administration environmental and healthcare policies
