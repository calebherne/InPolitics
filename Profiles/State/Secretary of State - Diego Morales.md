# Secretary of State - Diego Morales

## Basic Information
- **Name**: Diego Morales
- **Party**: Republican
- **Office**: Secretary of State of Indiana
- **Assumed Office**: January 1, 2023
- **Age**: 46 (born February 15, 1979)

## Background
- **Birthplace**: [Guatemala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guatemala)
- **Nationality**: Guatemalan-American (first Latino elected to statewide office in Indiana)
- **Generations in US**: 1st generation (immigrated to U.S. in high school with family)
- **Religion**: Not publicly disclosed
- **Education**:
  - [Silver Creek High School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silver_Creek_High_School_(Sellersburg,_Indiana)), Sellersburg, Indiana
  - [Indiana University Southeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indiana_University_Southeast) (BA, 2004)
  - [Purdue University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purdue_University) (MBA, 2012)
- **Military Service**: 
  - [U.S. Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army) active duty: 3 months, 18 days (2007)
  - [Indiana National Guard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indiana_National_Guard) (did not complete 8-year commitment)
  - Controversy: Described himself as "veteran" despite [not meeting federal definition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career) (requires active duty deployment)
- **Family**: Married to Sidonia (met while studying in Europe, she immigrated to U.S.); adult daughter from prior relationship
- **Employment History**: 
  - Advisor to Governor [Mike Pence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mike_Pence)
  - Secretary of State's Office (fired twice: 2009 under Todd Rokita for "incomplete work" and "lack of focus"; 2011 under Charles P. White after one month for "poor execution")

## Political Career Timeline
- **2018**: Ran for U.S. House (Indiana's 4th district), finished 3rd in Republican primary
- **2022**: Defeated incumbent [Holli Sullivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holli_Sullivan) at Republican convention for Secretary of State nomination
- **November 2022**: Elected Secretary of State (defeated Democrat Destiny Wells), underperformed other Republican statewide candidates by 5-7 percentage points
- **2023-present**: Secretary of State

## Immigration Stance
**Complex personal connection as immigrant**. Morales emphasizes his own legal immigration story:
- Self-describes: "I was born in Guatemala and legally immigrated with my family to the United States. I know the American Dream still exists because I am blessed to live it."
- Joined [America First Secretary of State Coalition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/America_First_Secretary_of_State_Coalition) during 2022 campaign (endorsed Trump's voter fraud claims), but later stated Biden won legitimately.
- Only "America First" coalition candidate to [win election in 2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career).
- No specific public policy positions on immigration found beyond personal narrative.

## What He's Known For

### Campaign Controversies (2022):
- **Military service claims**: Criticized for describing himself as "veteran" when he only [served 3 months active duty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career) and did not deploy. Federal veteran definition requires active duty service.

- **Campaign finance**: Used campaign funds to purchase [$43,000 Toyota RAV4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career).

- **Election claims flip-flop**: Joined America First coalition endorsing Trump's false fraud claims, then [told Washington Post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career) he believed Biden won legitimately.

- **Sexual assault allegations**: [Two women accused him](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Personal_life) of sexual assault. Morales denied the accusations.

- **Resume questions**: [Accused of exaggerating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career) political and military experience during 2018 congressional campaign.

- **Most negative press**: [WTHR commented](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career) he received most negative press of any Indiana statewide candidate since Richard Mourdock in 2012 Senate race.

### As Secretary of State (2023-present):
- **Nepotism**: Hired brother-in-law at [$108,000 salary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Tenure_as_Secretary_of_State).

- **No-bid contracts**: Office gave [millions in no-bid contracts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Tenure_as_Secretary_of_State) to companies that donated to his campaign.

- **Controversial spending**:
  - [$308,000 in spot bonuses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Tenure_as_Secretary_of_State) to nearly every employee (2023)
  - Purchased [$90,000 GMC Yukon Denali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Tenure_as_Secretary_of_State) with taxpayer funds from dealership that donated $65,000 to campaign

- **Foreign travel questions**:
  - **India trip**: Office claimed economic development trip despite that not being Secretary of State duty
  - **Hungary trip**: Attended [CPAC Hungary 2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Political_career), claimed "personal time" but visited government officials

- **2025 investigation**: [Marion County Election Board investigating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diego_Morales_(politician)#Tenure_as_Secretary_of_State) Morales over re-election video using footage from official visit to election center.

### Notable:
- First Latino elected to statewide office in Indiana
- Serves as Chief Elections Officer
- Oversees Business Services, Auto Dealer Services, Elections, and Securities divisions
